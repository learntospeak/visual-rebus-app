# Launch Checklist

## Product gate

- [ ] 120 playable puzzles and 30 reserve puzzles are approved.
- [ ] Every launch puzzle has three clues, accepted variants and an explanation.
- [ ] No placeholder text, clipped image, broken blank pattern or missing asset.
- [ ] No crash during a 30-puzzle session on at least three Android devices/sizes.
- [ ] Progress survives close/reopen, offline use and an app update.
- [ ] Wrong answers never consume paid currency automatically.

## Monetisation gate

- [ ] First 10 puzzles have no forced advertisement.
- [ ] Rewarded advertisements are optional and clearly described.
- [ ] Ad-free/supporter purchase states exactly what is removed.
- [ ] Ad-free users can obtain clue credits without watching ads.
- [ ] Restore purchases works after reinstall or device change where supported.
- [ ] No hidden “bonus game” contradicts the ad-free purchase wording.

## Privacy and accessibility gate

- [ ] Privacy policy lists every SDK and data use.
- [ ] Store data declarations match actual behaviour.
- [ ] Support email and version number are visible.
- [ ] Large text, high contrast and reduced motion work.
- [ ] Important buttons and puzzle elements have screen-reader labels.
- [ ] Sound and haptics can be disabled.

## Google Play preparation

- [ ] Confirm account testing eligibility and requirements.
- [ ] Recruit at least 12 testers early when the closed-test rule applies.
- [ ] Prepare unique icon, feature graphic and phone screenshots.
- [ ] Complete Data safety and content-rating forms.
- [ ] Test billing, ads, consent and restore flows using test accounts.

## Store page sequence

1. **Screenshot 1:** a genuinely solvable visual puzzle — “Can you get it in 10 seconds?”
2. **Screenshot 2:** the progressive clue ladder.
3. **Screenshot 3:** the satisfying step-by-step explanation.
4. **Screenshot 4:** daily challenge, streak and sharing.
5. **Screenshot 5:** mixed visual formats and offline play.

## Marketing launch week

- [ ] Publish one short puzzle video per day.
- [ ] Keep the answer hidden initially; invite comments.
- [ ] Record one family, workplace or street challenge.
- [ ] Contact 20 small puzzle/trivia/family creators; test 3–5 collaborations.
- [ ] Reuse the best-performing organic video before buying broader ads.
- [ ] Do not spend heavily until Day-7 retention and revenue per retained player are known.

## First 30-day decision

Continue investing only when players return and rate puzzles as fair. Prioritise:

- Tutorial completion: target 80%+.
- First-session puzzles solved: median 5+.
- Day-1 return: aim for 30%+ in early tests.
- Day-7 return: aim for 10–15%+ initially.
- Fair rating: target 85%.
- Share rate: target 1–3%+ of solved sessions.

Treat these as directional early targets, not guarantees.
