# Market Research Notes

Research snapshot: 15 July 2026. Store figures can change.

## Market conclusion

The category has proven demand but is not empty. A cleaner interface alone is insufficient because newer competitors already offer pictures, colour, daily puzzles, streaks and multiplayer. The strongest opening is **trust and satisfaction**:

- fair, region-aware answers;
- progressive clues that help rather than waste currency;
- clear explanation after every solve;
- transparent one-time monetisation;
- reliable progress and purchases;
- a lightweight, polished mobile experience.

## Competitor snapshot

### Dingbats — Between the Lines

**Visible strength:** 1M+ Google Play downloads, large review base, long-running content library.

**Weaknesses to counter:**

- mixed and dated presentation;
- regional phrase friction;
- reviews describing unclear or tedious puzzles;
- hints that may provide poor value;
- multiple ad-removal price points and monetisation confusion;
- user reports of progress or purchase problems;
- mixed game modes dilute the core identity;
- broad tracking/privacy disclosures and no declared Apple accessibility support at the research date.

### Mister Rebus

**Visible strength:** 100K+ Google Play downloads, colourful presentation, offline play and coin hints.

**Weaknesses to counter:** basic value proposition, advertising/tracking disclosures and little visible explanation-first differentiation.

### Rebus Puzzles — Brain Teasers

**Visible strength:** 10K+ Google Play downloads, hundreds of puzzles, difficulty levels and hints.

**Weaknesses to counter:** generic store positioning and weak visible social, daily and explanation story.

### Rebus Quest

**Visible strength:** picture riddles, packs, XP, daily goals and polished progression.

**Weaknesses to counter:** limited visible rating traction, large package size and no obvious explanation-first advantage.

### RapidRebus

**Visible strength:** strongest direct feature benchmark — 1,000+ claimed handcrafted puzzles, daily mode, multiplayer, leaderboards, offline play, themes and accessibility.

**Weaknesses to counter:** very low visible store traction despite the feature set, showing that features and polish do not automatically create distribution.

### Riddles, Rebuses and Two Pics

**Visible strength:** broad mixed puzzle library.

**Weaknesses to counter:** diluted rebus identity, older presentation and low visible review volume.

## What not to copy

- Another app's brand, icon, screenshots, exact UI, source code or puzzle collection.
- A near-identical reskin with only new colours.
- Misleading “remove ads” wording.
- Obscure answers presented as difficulty.
- Random stock-art mixtures without a coherent visual system.
- Multiple unrelated mini-games before the core product is proven.
